How NLP Can Contribute to Society and Sustainable Development

NLP-driven systems like our Marathi hospital chatbot directly advance language accessibility: a large share of patients in Maharashtra are more comfortable communicating in Marathi than English, and a chatbot that understands their own language removes a real barrier to healthcare access — especially for elderly patients and those from rural or semi-urban backgrounds who may hesitate to approach hospital staff in English.

This also supports regional language technology more broadly. Every dataset, stemmer, or embedding model built for Marathi in a project like this adds to the still-thin pool of NLP resources for Indian languages, which today remain underserved compared to English. Small, applied projects like ours contribute real, if modest, momentum toward closing that gap.

The project supports educational inclusion and digital literacy indirectly — patients unfamiliar with hospital procedures learn to interact with a simple conversational interface rather than a complex portal or form. It also functions as an assistive technology for patients with mobility limitations or anxiety about in-person queries, letting them get answers without physically waiting in line.

Finally, this is a clear case of improving citizen/patient services: reducing reception queue times frees hospital staff to focus on higher-value care tasks, and cuts patient waiting time — a small but meaningful efficiency gain that scales across every patient interaction, day after day, with no additional infrastructure cost once deployed.

  • Language Accessibility — Marathi-first interaction for non-English-comfortable patients
  • Regional Language Technologies — contributes to the Marathi NLP resource ecosystem
  • Educational Inclusion — simplifies navigating hospital procedures
  • Assistive Technologies — reduces need for in-person queries
  • Citizen/Patient Services — lowers waiting time and staff workload
